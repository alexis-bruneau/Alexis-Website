Evaluating Classifiers for Equity Performance Direction

The work done in this project aimed to explore machine learning approaches for predicting equity performance in the stock market. The study focused on evaluating three classifier models: Random Forest (RF), Multi-Layer Perceptron (MLP), and Support Vector Machines (SVM). Through an extensive analysis that included hyperparameter tuning, sensitivity studies, and explainability using feature importance analysis, the project found that RF emerged as the best-performing model, with strong generalization abilities and suitability for explainability techniques. The project also highlighted the challenges of predicting stock performance and emphasized the importance of data preprocessing and exploratory data analysis. Future work directions include exploring more deep learning architectures, studying additional market datasets, and considering regression problems to capture the magnitude of stock value changes. The findings of this project contribute to the growing field of machine learning in stock market prediction and provide valuable insights for both researchers and investors.
