Perception and Point Cloud Registration

This project encompassed two critical aspects: Feature Point Detection and Correspondences, and 3D Point Cloud Registration.

In the first phase, Feature Point Detection and Correspondences, the objective entailed the detection and alignment of feature points in stereo image pairs derived from the KITTI Dataset The ORB detector and descriptor were deployed for identifying 1000 key points per image pair. These key points and their corresponding descriptors were then meticulously matched between the left and right images, adhering to an epipolar constraint. The project also included an analysis of the appearance of unreliable key points, with a specific emphasis on those in less illuminated areas of the images.

The second phase, 3D Point Cloud Registration, was dedicated to the alignment of 3D point clouds utilizing (ICP) algorithm on the Stanford 3D Scanning Repository. The procedure of nearest neighbor search was conducted to discern the optimal matches between the source and target point clouds. The ICP algorithm progressively fine-tuned the alignment by estimating a 6D pose that minimized the Euclidean distance between matched points. Singular Value Decomposition (SVD) was employed for pose estimation, and the mean Euclidean distance was computed to assess the quality of alignment.

At its core, the project was designed to demonstrate the intricate process of feature point detection, matching, and 3D point cloud registration techniques. This involved the application of complex algorithms such as ORB, Lowe's ratio test, RANSAC, and SVD, with the ultimate goal of achieving accurate alignment and registration of the point clouds. The evaluation was carried out based on the accuracy of the estimated poses and the alignment of the registered point clouds.
