Evaluating Preprocessing Alternatives for Ore-Waste Classification of Hyperspectral Images

This paper presents my thesis project conducted at the University of Toronto, a study in the realm of hyperspectral imaging for mineral mapping. My research offers a thorough evaluation of preprocessing techniques and segmentation models, tailored to address the computational strains of image segmentation tasks in mineral applications.

Employing these methods on a substantial dataset curated for mineral mapping, this investigation reveals how Principal Component Analysis (PCA) dramatically enhances data manageability, reducing computational time by a factor of five while simultaneously improving model accuracy by 5% . The outcomes not only highlight the efficiency of PCA but also the superior capabilities of cutting-edge segmentation models such as U-Net++ and FPN. Together, these advancements demonstrate a strategic compromise between computational speed and precision, paving the way for continued advancements in large-scale hyperspectral imaging for mineral exploration.
