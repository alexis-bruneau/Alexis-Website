Automated Essay Scoring

An end-to-end pipeline was developed to autonomously grade 8th to 12th student essays across the following criteria cohesion, syntax, vocabulary, phraseology, grammar, and conventions. Leveraging advanced pre-trained transformer-based neural language models including Deberta, BERT, and Hugging Face Transformers, the project involved significant work in preprocessing and cleaning a dataset of English essays from students in grades 8-12. The dataset can be found here Kaggle Dataset.

The project involved intricate data manipulation techniques, such as stemming, stop word removal and vocabulary reduction. Notably, the project also involved careful handling of long essays through various text length control methods, along with data rebalancing and tokenization for optimal model performance. The project demanded the design and implementation of a multi-approach pooling method for effective feature extraction. This was complemented by a custom-built neural network architecture comprising two dense layers for regression. A Multi-level hyperparameter tuning played a crucial role in reducing the model's loss. Our model achieved competitive results with the Kaggle competition winnner. A presentation of our project can be found in the video underneath.
