RRT vs RRT*

This project was designed to delve into sampling-based motion planning, specifically utilizing rapidly-exploring random trees (RRT) algorithms. The task involved developing motion planning solutions for a Dubins-type vehicle, a model widely used in robotics, which has restrictions on the path's curvature and assumes the vehicle can only move forward.

Two separate motion planning algorithms were implemented: a standard RRT-based planner and an RRT*-based planner. Both these planners were developed to navigate a 2D planar world with circular obstacles, with each planner accepting the same problem setup including the world map, list of obstacles, start pose, and goal pose. The objective was to devise a list of nodes defining a valid path from the start to the goal state.

The project concluded with a comparative performance analysis of the RRT and RRT* planners, visualized through a bar chart plot of 1,000 random instances of a challenging Dubins planning problem. The analysis focused on the average path length to highlight the efficiency gains made possible by the use of RRT*.

Average Cost & Computational Time

In this section, RRT and RRT* was tested for 1000 simulations and its average cost and computational time were compared. The results were as expected where the average cost was higher for RRT with a score of 50 and RRT* having 32. On the other side, RRT computational time was much lower with average time of 0.06 seconds per iteration while RRT* had an average time of 0.3 seconds.
