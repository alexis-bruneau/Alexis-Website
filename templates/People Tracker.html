A YOLOv7-Based Multiple Object Tracking Approach

In this study, we present an Enhanced People Flow Management system using a YOLOv7-based Multiple Object Tracking (MOT) approach. Amid the post-COVID19 societal reopening, managing crowd density becomes crucial to prevent incidents caused by overcrowding. To address this, we have developed an algorithm to detect, track, and count individuals in real-time, specifically designed for indoor environments like stores, restaurants, and concert venues. The model leverages YOLOv7 for detecting people and generating bounding boxes, which then facilitates multiple object tracking between video frames. Furthermore, we have implemented bidirectional tracking to differentiate incoming and outgoing traffic accurately. The system is trained using the MOT15 Dataset, MOT15 dataset, achieving a MOTA score of 94.3% and a MOTP score of 39.1%.
